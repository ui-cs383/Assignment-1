Computer Adaptation

Section Contributors

Jeff Crocker
Benjamin Cumber
Andrew Schwartzmeyer

The goal of the computer adaptation should be to free the players of the tedious mathematics and management, while maintaining the charming aspects and the intentional design and function. Below is a list of the components and their proposed adaptations.

  • Physical Components
  • Gameplay Mechanics

Game Board

  • Environ

    Environ are the smallest unit of the gameboard, and thus present a challenge for adaptation as screen size and resolution is an issue. An auto-zoom mechanism may be a possible solution for this. The adaptation should mimic the board's display of Environ information. Making the battle setting skinnable to match the location would add depth to the interface.

  • Planets

    Planets should mimic the board design; however, there are some places digital adapatation may save us space and thus shrink the size of the game map. For instance, the loyalty scale uses much space as a physical system, but with the substitution of a color scheme and representative icon could be cut down to one square. The same can be said for other planet characteristics.

  • Star Systems

    Star systems will not need to be changed much graphically, only whatever is needed to suit planetary changes.

  • Provinces

    Provinces will need to be adapted to allow for a jigsaw puzzle-like size and fit of each individual province. This is because, due to screen limitations, players will likely only be able to view one province at a time, and will need to navigate between them from a large galaxy map.

  • Galaxy

    The map of the galaxy will need to be simplified and scaled down to fit, and likely only present pertinent macro information, bringing player's attention to issues and then zooming to smaller scale view to actually do micro management.

Pieces

  • Character Pieces

    Character pieces can be very similar to the table implementation. When viewing the game board, the player cannot see into the stacks at various planets any details of the individuals, until they pick up the pieces for examination. To mimic this behavior, we need to display only the quantity and total group strength of the entire stack, until the player 'picks up' a piece. We will then display an 'examination view' that shows the exact composition, and links to the corresponding cards.

  • Standard Military

    Standard military will be shown in a similar fashion, although with fewer details.

Cards

  • Characters and Ships

    A large part of the game's "charm", which we would like to preserve, comes from these cards. For this reason it should be a goal to provide character portraits, item views, etc. for all cards just as is done in the board game. These "cards" will be presented to the player as the more detailed view of the pieces on the map.

  • Missions

    Mission related cards, on the other hand, are not present on the board, but rather are composed of reactionary information. We picture it being adapted as a "news bulletin" that would pop up to the player whenever needed, such as before each turn.

  • Galactic Events

    Galactic events may also be a part of this "Galactic News Bulletin", and any effects remaining for the duration of the turn may stay pinned in a portion of the screen dedicated to meta-information, with an expiration countdown for any timed effects.

Combat

Dice rolling for ALL combat will not commence until the player explicitly performs it. The dice outcome will be handled by a random number generator.

  • Character Combat

    Combat between characters can be shown using the detailed "card" view described previously. Both cards would then be used to model participation in combat.

  • Environ Military Combat

    Military battles should be set in the appropriate Environ. There should be some aesthetic battle representation.

  • Space Military Combat

    Space combat would be similarly adapted, but obviously with different graphics representing the scene and units.

Movement

  • Hyperspace Movement

    A graphical representation showing the origin and destination will remain on the map until the hyperspace jump is acknowledged by the player. An animated movement routine would make this more exciting.

  • Interplanetary Movement

    Movement between planets will be shown by moving unit stacks, with a trail from the path displayed to provide context to the player.

  • Planetary Movement

    Movement between Enivorns will be tricky to display clearly considering the scale of the map, so as with other movement there is a need for an iconic representation (or perhaps news bulletin pop-up) to alert the players that a move has taken place.

Missions

  • Mission Assignment

    There will be a mission manager window which will be brought up at the proper phases, displaying all active characters, their assigned missions, progress, and other relevant information.

  • Mission Actions

    Actions will be made using the same "mission manager" window.

  • Mission Bonus

    Bonuses that can be applied to active missions will present the mission manager to the user and prompt for where to assign the bonus.

Systems

  • Resource Management

    At the proper phase, both players will be given financial reports and prompted as to what policies they would like to put in place for future.

  • Turn 'State'

    Process which lists the current turn, phase, state, etc. should be present in a meta-information overall and therefore generally available for player to reference.

  • Search

    Searching will be a computation phase for the game. There is no need to present the players with the failed searches. Rather, it will prompt a "Galactic News Bulletin", and consequences will be applied.
